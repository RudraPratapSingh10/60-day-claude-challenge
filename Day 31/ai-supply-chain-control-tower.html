How the Control Tower Works

Alerts appear on the board as real operational problems: port delays, broken trucks, supplier failures, and more. Each has a priority level and a countdown — if it hits zero before you act, it resolves itself badly.

Priority Colors

  • Red · Critical — resolve immediately, big consequences if missed.
  • Orange · High — act soon, meaningful KPI impact.
  • Blue · Medium — lower urgency, still matters.

Your Actions

  • Each alert offers 2–3 tailored responses plus generic Ignore and Delay options.
  • The best response improves KPIs and Score, protects revenue, and reduces risk.
  • Weak or mismatched responses cost you — higher operating cost, lower satisfaction.
  • Delay gives a brief reprieve but can trigger a worse follow-up a few seconds later.
  • Ignore is fastest, but almost always the costliest choice.

Winning the Shift

Keep Service Level, Customer Satisfaction, Inventory Health, and Transportation Efficiency high while keeping Operating Cost low and Revenue Protected climbing. Your final grade (A+ to D) is based on total score across all 180 seconds.
